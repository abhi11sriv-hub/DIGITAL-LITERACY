# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this? If not, don't post it.

2. **Keep your profiles consistent and professional** — Use a real name, a clear profile photo, and an appropriate bio on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub.

3. **Use privacy settings** — Regularly review and update the privacy settings on personal social media accounts (Instagram, Facebook, Snapchat) to control who can see your content.

4. **Credit others for their work** — If you share someone else's image, article, or idea, always tag or credit the original creator.

5. **Engage respectfully and constructively** — Comment thoughtfully, disagree politely, and avoid trolling or heated arguments online.

6. **Report harmful content** — If you encounter cyberbullying, misinformation, or inappropriate content, use the platform's reporting feature instead of engaging with it.

7. **Separate personal and professional accounts** — Keep a professional presence (LinkedIn, GitHub) distinct from purely personal platforms to maintain credibility with future employers.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your phone number, home address, daily routine, or financial information on public profiles.

2. **Don't share or forward unverified news** — Always fact-check information before resharing. Spreading misinformation, even unintentionally, damages your credibility.

3. **Don't post offensive, discriminatory, or inflammatory content** — Racist, sexist, or hateful posts can permanently harm your reputation and have real-world consequences including expulsion or job rejection.

4. **Don't overshare location information in real time** — Posting live location updates (e.g., "at the airport right now") can compromise your safety.

5. **Don't engage with or respond to cyberbullies** — Responding to online harassment often escalates the situation. Screenshot, report, and block instead.

6. **Don't use social media during exams or class** — Not only does it hurt your academic performance, it can be seen as disrespectful and unprofessional.

7. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers indiscriminately** — Be selective about who has access to your personal posts and information.

---

*Remember: Your digital footprint is permanent. What you post today can be found years later by employers, academic institutions, and colleagues.*
