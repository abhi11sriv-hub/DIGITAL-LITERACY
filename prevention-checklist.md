# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
## For College Students in India

---

### 🔐 General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** — Create passwords with at least 12 characters including letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across multiple accounts. Use a password manager (e.g., Bitwarden) to manage them.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** — Activate 2FA on all important accounts (email, banking, social media). This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Keep your devices and apps updated** — Software updates often include critical security patches. Always install updates promptly on your phone, laptop, and apps.

4. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks** — Never log in to banking apps or enter personal information while connected to public or unsecured Wi-Fi. Use mobile data instead.

5. **Verify before you click** — Do not click on links in messages or emails from unknown senders. Hover over links to check the actual destination URL before clicking.

6. **Download apps only from official sources** — Only install apps from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown websites.

---

### 💸 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Understand the difference between "Send" and "Receive" on UPI** — A UPI collect request (incoming request) asks YOU to authorize a debit from your account. NEVER accept a collect request from a stranger. Receiving money does NOT require you to enter your PIN.

8. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — No bank, government official, or customer support representative will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP. Anyone who does is a scammer — disconnect immediately.

9. **Verify the UPI ID before every transaction** — Always double-check the recipient's name that appears on screen after entering their UPI ID before confirming any payment.

10. **Be cautious when selling items online (OLX, Facebook Marketplace)** — Fraudsters posing as buyers often send collect requests and claim "I've sent the money." Always check your actual bank balance before handing over any item.

---

### 🚨 Where to Report Cybercrime

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24x7)
- **RBI Complaint Portal (for financial fraud):** [cms.rbi.org.in](https://cms.rbi.org.in)
- **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline:** Call immediately to freeze transactions if you suspect fraud

---

*Report cybercrime promptly — the sooner you report, the higher the chance of recovering funds and catching the perpetrator.*
