# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Cybercrime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

---

## Case Study

### Title: The "Refund Scam" — How Ravi Lost ₹45,000 in 10 Minutes

**Background:**
Ravi, a 20-year-old engineering student from Bhopal, had recently listed his old laptop for sale on OLX. Within hours, he received a message from a buyer claiming to be an Army officer stationed in Pune.

**How It Happened (Step-by-Step):**

1. The "buyer" contacted Ravi and agreed to pay the full asking price of ₹45,000 without negotiating.
2. He told Ravi he would send the payment via Google Pay (UPI) and needed Ravi's UPI ID.
3. A few minutes later, Ravi received a UPI notification — but instead of a credit, it was a **payment request** (a collect request). The scammer had told him "I've sent the money, please accept it on your UPI app."
4. Ravi, unfamiliar with the difference between a payment request and a payment received, accepted the request — unknowingly authorizing the scammer to pull ₹45,000 from his account.
5. The scammer immediately blocked Ravi on all platforms and became unreachable.

**Who Is Targeted:**
First-time online sellers, students, elderly individuals, and anyone unfamiliar with how UPI collect requests work are the most common targets.

**Consequences:**
Ravi lost ₹45,000 in a matter of minutes. Despite filing a complaint on cybercrime.gov.in and calling helpline 1930, recovering the funds was extremely difficult as the scammer had already withdrawn the money using a mule account. The emotional stress and financial setback significantly impacted Ravi's semester.

**Source:** This is a realistic fictional scenario based on widely reported patterns of OLX-related UPI fraud in India, documented by the Reserve Bank of India and cybercrime awareness campaigns.
